Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is a digital security certificate?
A digital security certificate is an electronic document used to prove the ownership of a public key. It is issued by a trusted Certificate Authority (CA) and helps secure online communications.
Why do I need a digital security certificate?
Digital certificates enable secure data transmission by encrypting information and verifying the identity of websites or entities, protecting against data breaches and fraud.
How do I get a digital security certificate?
You can obtain a digital security certificate from a trusted Certificate Authority by submitting a certificate signing request (CSR) and completing the validation process.
What types of digital certificates are available?
Common types include SSL/TLS certificates for websites, code signing certificates, email certificates, and client authentication certificates.
How long is a digital certificate valid?
The validity period varies but typically ranges from 1 to 3 years, after which the certificate must be renewed.
What happens if my digital certificate expires?
If a certificate expires, browsers will warn users that the site is not secure. It is important to renew certificates before expiration to maintain trust and security.
Can I use the same certificate for multiple websites?
Some certificates, like wildcard or multi-domain certificates, allow securing multiple websites or subdomains under a single certificate.
